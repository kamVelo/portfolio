Who am I?

I'm a 17 year-old sixth form student in my final year of secondary school at Leicester Grammar School. I'm studying for four A Levels: Maths, Further Maths, Physics and Economics. I'm also doing an Extended-Project-Qualification which you can learn more about on the home page. I'm interested in pursuing a career in finance or computing but ideally a combination of the two and ultimately I'd like to start my own fintech firm probably trading in complex financial products but thats a long way down my career path.

My Interests:

  • Philosophy - I've been focusing a lot on Nietzsche lately which has led me down really interesting intellectual paths with other philosophers too such as Schopenhauer.
  • Economics - I'm interested in learning about market solutions to social problems facing developed countries and how we can reduce government involvement in the long run to a minimum through more effective policy. In addition, I'm academically passionate about the conflict between the Austrian and Chicago schools and Keynesian economics. Believing in the Austrian school myself, I'm interested in how individuals and small organisations can help to create decentralised systems based on the principles of voluntarism.
  • Machine Learning - I've used machine learning in lots of my projects but I've mostly used it in a black-box fashion, so I'd really like to learn about different machine learning models especially those applied in sequential problems. I'd also really like to get to grips with the calculus involved in back-propagation.
  • Finance - I'm fascinated by finance as a sector, I'm particularly enamoured by the financial engineering of standardised structures and products that solve very specific problems for a lot of agents and provide value to all parties. Reading "Money and Power" By William D. Cohan and learning about some of the traders involved in saving Goldman Sachs from massive losses in 2008 through their skillful use of complex financial products to position themselves strongly is absolutely awe-inspiring to me and only furthered my desire to pursue a career somehow intertwined with both finance and computing.
  • Engineering - Designing and making things of all kinds is something I want to do a lot more of, when I have the time and the freedom such as in university, it's something I'm going to spend a lot of time doing.
